📚 Reading Intervention

Help your child fall in love
with reading

These are the exact strategies Ms. Mary uses at the hub and recommends to every parent.

01

Reading · Ages 3–8

Read Aloud Every Day

Reading aloud is one of the single most powerful things you can do for a child's literacy. It builds vocabulary, comprehension, and most importantly — a love of stories. Even 10 minutes a day makes a lasting difference.

1
Pick a book slightly above your child's reading level
2
Make it cosy — same time, same spot each day
3
Use voices, pause, and ask "what do you think happens next?"
4
Let them choose the book sometimes

02

Reading · All Ages

Build a Home Library — Any Size

Children who grow up surrounded by books read more — regardless of how many books there are. Even a shelf with 10 well-chosen books creates a "reading environment" in the home that shapes a child's identity as a reader.

1
Start with 5–10 books at the child's eye level
2
Include a mix: stories, facts, Bible
3
Rotate books — visit the library if possible
4
Let the child "own" their shelf

03

Reading Intervention

When Your Child Struggles — 5 Signs to Watch For

Early reading difficulties are highly treatable when caught early. Here are the signs that a child may need reading intervention:

!
Avoids reading aloud or pretends to read
!
Reverses letters frequently past age 7
!
Reads slowly and loses their place often
!
Strong orally but struggles when reading independently
!
Gets frustrated or emotional around reading

If you notice these, WhatsApp us — we can help.

✍️ Handwriting Intervention

Handwriting tips that
actually work

01

Grip & Posture

Fix the Grip First

A poor pencil grip affects not just handwriting but speed and stamina. The correct grip is the "dynamic tripod" — thumb, index, and middle finger, holding 2–3 cm from the tip. Correct early — it's much harder to fix later.

02

Letter Formation

Start from the Correct Entry Point

Most handwriting problems stem from incorrect starting points and stroke direction. Teach letters in families — letters that start the same way (c, a, d, g, q all start from the same curved stroke). Use sky-writing to practice formation in the air first.

03

Practice Strategy

Little & Often Beats Long Sessions

5–10 minutes of focused handwriting practice daily is far more effective than one 45-minute session a week. Use wide-ruled paper for young writers, and never force a child to fill a whole page — quality over quantity every time.

🗓️ Routines

Routines that raise
confident learners

🌅 Morning Routine (20 min)

5 minPrayer or short devotional
5 minSpeak 3 affirmations over your child
5 minReview what they'll learn today
5 minSend them off with enthusiasm

🌙 After-School Routine (45 min)

10 minSnack + free time to decompress
20 minHomework (while you're nearby)
10 minRead aloud together
5 minTalk about their day — no screens

📖 Weekend Reading Habit

30 minIndependent reading (no interruptions)
10 minTell me 3 things about your book
5 minDraw a character or favourite scene

💬 For Teachers: Classroom Tips

📌Start every lesson with 2–3 praise affirmations
📌Use activity-based teaching — not just talking
📌Call every child by name at least once each lesson
📌End class with "what did I learn today?"
🌍 Annual Event

World Read Aloud Day

Every February, D'Marie Vivre Learning Hub joins the global celebration of reading aloud as one of the most powerful literacy tools available to parents, teachers, and children everywhere.

Reading aloud is not just for young children. It builds comprehension, expands vocabulary, develops a sense of story, and — most importantly — it creates joyful, connected moments between children and the adults who love them.

Join the Campaign

Why it matters

Children who are read to regularly arrive at school with a vocabulary up to 3 years ahead of peers who are not. The impact is measurable — and it starts at home.

How to participate

Read aloud to a child on World Read Aloud Day (February). Share a photo with #WorldReadAloudDay or tag us @dvlearninghub. It's that simple.
